Theme Guide
How to plan the perfect Spy Adventure birthday for your child
Secret missions, hidden clues, decoded invitations — the Spy theme is one of the most immersive we do. Here's everything you need to pull it off brilliantly.
🕵️ Theme Guide  ·  5 min read
What makes the Spy theme special: The Spy Adventure theme works because it gives every child a role — they're agents on a mission. Activities like the laser maze, fingerprint station, code-cracking puzzles, and the Spy briefing keep kids engaged for 2–3 hours without a dull moment.

Decor essentials: Dark panels with neon green accents, classified files, mission dossiers as name tags, a "spy HQ" entrance arch. Our standard Spy package includes 150–200 balloons, 1 feature wall, and 2 side panels — all in mission black + lime green.

Best for: Boys aged 6–12. Works beautifully at home, a community hall, or a banquet. Minimum 15 kids recommended for the activity flow to feel exciting.

Pro tip: Send e-invitations designed as "classified mission briefings" 10 days in advance — kids arrive already in character. Plan a Spy Birthday →
Gift Ideas
Top 10 return gift ideas kids actually love in 2025
Return gifts are the last impression your party leaves. Skip the generic stationery — here are ideas that make kids' eyes light up when they reach home.
🎁 Gift Guide  ·  4 min read
The rule: personalise, don't genericise The best return gifts feel curated. A good gift should be usable, edible, or memorable. Our top 10:

1. Theme-matched activity kits — spy decoder, art kit, science experiment
2. Personalised mini notebooks with the birthday child's name + theme
3. Seed bomb sets — eco, unique, great for ages 4+
4. Chocolate bark boxes — custom designed with the party theme
5. Slime-making kits — universally loved by ages 5–10
6. Custom tattoo sets — themed temporary tattoos are a hit
7. Mini Lego sets — small, premium, always wanted
8. Sensory play bags — great for toddler parties (ages 1–4)
9. Personalized water bottles — practical and used long after the party
10. DIY cookie decorating kits — kids love a creative project to take home

Budget sweet spot: ₹200–₹400 per gift bag. Going above ₹600 per bag rarely adds more joy — invest the extra in an activity instead.
Checklist
The complete birthday planning checklist — 8 weeks to party day
Whether you're DIY-ing it or working with a team like ours, this timeline keeps you ahead of every deadline and stress-free on the day.
📋 Planning Guide  ·  6 min read
8 weeks out: Fix the date, shortlist venues, decide on a rough theme and budget. Book your venue as soon as possible — popular spots fill up fast on weekends in Mumbai and Pune.

6 weeks out: Confirm the venue, finalise your theme, and book your main vendors. If you're using Wondershop, build your plan in the builder — takes under 10 minutes.

4 weeks out: Send invitations. Finalise guest list. Order the cake — give your baker at least 3 weeks for a custom design.

2 weeks out: Confirm RSVPs, finalise headcount with your vendor, plan the day-of timeline (arrival, setup, party start, cake, activities, food, return gifts).

1 week out: Pack a party kit — sellotape, scissors, safety pins, a printed copy of every vendor's number. Confirm setup time. Brief the birthday child on what to expect.

Day before: Charge your phone. Prep the birthday outfit. Rest.

Party day: Arrive 30 minutes before guests. Let your team handle the rest. Your only job is to be present and enjoy it. Build your birthday plan →
