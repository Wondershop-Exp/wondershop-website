Gift Ideas

Top 10 return gift ideas kids actually love in 2026

🎁 Gift Guide · 4 min read · By Shruti Garg, Wondershop
← Back to Blog

Return gifts are the last impression your birthday party makes. A thoughtful return gift says: this party was special, and so are you. A generic one says... nothing much at all.

After planning 500+ parties across Mumbai, we've seen what lands and what ends up forgotten in the back of a cupboard. Here are the 10 return gift ideas that consistently make kids' eyes light up when they reach home.

The one rule: personalise, don't genericise The best return gifts feel like they were chosen for this party — not pulled from a standard box. Even a small touch of personalisation (a name tag, a theme-matched wrapper, a handwritten note from the birthday child) elevates the simplest gift into a memory.

The top 10

1

Theme-matched activity kits

A spy decoder kit for a Spy party. A science experiment pack for a Science theme. A paint-your-own kit for an Art party. When the return gift matches the experience, the whole day comes home with the child. These work for ages 5 and up.

Best for ages 5–12
2

Personalised mini notebooks

A small hardcover notebook with the birthday child's name and theme printed on the cover. Kids use these for everything — drawing, journalling, secret codes. Practical and personal.

Budget-friendly
3

Slime-making kits

Universally loved by ages 5–10. A small pouch with pre-measured ingredients, glitter, and instructions. Kids make it at home, which extends the party excitement by another day. Messy, but magnificently popular.

Ages 5–10
4

Custom chocolate bark boxes

Chocolate bark printed or decorated with the party theme. Edible, beautiful, and genuinely exciting to receive. The parents love these almost as much as the children. Order from a local chocolatier for best results.

All ages
5

Sensory play bags (for toddler parties)

For first birthdays and parties for under-4s, sensory bags are perfect. A ziplock or small pouch with kinetic sand, textured items, or water beads. Simple to assemble, age-appropriate, and safe.

Ages 1–4
6

Seed bomb sets

Eco-friendly and unique — a small collection of seed bombs (compressed soil + seeds that can be tossed into a garden) with a little card explaining how they work. Great for eco-conscious families. Stand out from the usual party bag completely.

Ages 4+
7

Personalised water bottles

A stainless steel or BPA-free water bottle with the child's name printed on it. Used every single day. Parents especially appreciate the practicality, which means they'll remember your party every morning at the school drop-off.

All ages
8

Mini Lego sets

Small, premium, always wanted. The 30-piece Lego Creator or City sets retail for ₹250–₹400 and feel like a real gift rather than a filler. Kids who already have Lego love additions; kids who don't have Lego now want more.

Ages 4–12
9

Custom temporary tattoo sets

Theme-matched temporary tattoos — spy badges, princess crowns, dinosaurs, football crests — are inexpensive, easy to apply, and produce enormous excitement. Kids apply them immediately and compare. Order custom-designed sets for a fully branded feel.

Ages 3–10
10

DIY cookie decorating kits

A small box with 2–3 plain sugar cookies, icing in a squeeze bottle, and sprinkles. Kids decorate them at home — it becomes a whole other activity after the party. Works beautifully for younger ages and baking-themed parties.

Ages 3–9

Budget guidance

The sweet spot for return gifts in Mumbai is ₹200–₹400 per bag. That's enough to include 2–3 items (one main gift + a sweet + a small sticker or tattoo) without going overboard.

A note on spending more Going above ₹600 per bag rarely adds more joy for the guest. The extra ₹200 is almost always better spent on one more activity or a slightly bigger cake. Children remember what they did far more than what they received.

How many bags to prepare

Always prepare 10–15% more bags than your RSVP count. Surprise plus-ones are common at kids' parties, and running out of return gifts is a stress you don't need on the day.

Presentation matters

Even a simple gift feels special in the right packaging. A brown kraft paper bag with the party theme's colours and a ribbon, or a small themed box with the guest's name written on it, makes the gift feel intentional. Add a handwritten note or a sticker from the birthday child for a personal touch that costs almost nothing.
